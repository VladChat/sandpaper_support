Poor Results Between Coats

Sanding between paint, primer, sealer, or clear coats leaves visible problems.

paintprimerclear coatwood finishtoo coarse

The grit is too coarse between coats

Likely cause
A coarse grit removed too much coating or left scratches that show through the next layer.
Recommended grit
Use fine grits such as 320–600 depending on coating stage and finish goal.
Wet or dry
Light dry sanding or wet sanding for finer finish stages where appropriate.
Success check
The surface feels smooth and lightly prepared without cutting through the coating.

What to do

  1. Let the coating cure according to the coating instructions.
  2. Use light pressure.
  3. Sand only enough to smooth and prepare the surface.
  4. Clean the surface fully before the next coat.
Avoid: Do not use heavy-removal grits between finish coats.
