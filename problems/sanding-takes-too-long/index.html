Sanding Takes Too Long

The sandpaper is cutting slowly or the task feels inefficient.

wrong gritworn sheethard coatingheavy removal

The starting grit is too fine

Likely cause
A fine grit is being used for a removal job that needs a coarser grit first.
Recommended grit
Use 60–120 for heavy removal, then step up to finer grits.
Wet or dry
Dry sanding is usually faster for early removal.
Success check
Material removal becomes faster without needing excessive pressure.

What to do

  1. Switch to a coarser grit suited to the removal stage.
  2. Sand evenly until the defect is reduced.
  3. Move to the next finer grit to refine the surface.
  4. Replace worn sheets promptly.
Avoid: Do not use 400+ grit for heavy removal.
