Sandpaper Clogs Too Fast

Dust, paint, finish, or residue loads into the abrasive surface quickly.

paintsoft finishwood dustwet sandingdry sanding

Paper clogs while sanding paint or finish

Likely cause
Soft paint, finish residue, or dust buildup is loading the abrasive surface.
Recommended grit
Use the correct prep grit for the stage; move to wet sanding on finer grits when appropriate.
Wet or dry
Dry for removal; wet for fine finish stages to reduce loading.
Success check
The sheet continues cutting and the surface does not smear or gum up.

What to do

  1. Clean the surface before sanding.
  2. Use lighter pressure.
  3. Wipe dust or residue often.
  4. Replace the sheet when cutting slows.
  5. Use wet sanding for finer finishing where suitable.
Avoid: Do not keep sanding with a fully loaded sheet.
