Wet Sanding Leaves Haze

Wet sanding leaves a cloudy, dull, or hazy surface.

clear coatpaintplasticfine gritpolishing prep

Haze remains after fine wet sanding

Likely cause
Fine wet sanding normally leaves a uniform dull haze that must be refined with finer grits and then polished where the project requires gloss.
Recommended grit
Progress through finer grits, commonly 1000 → 1500 → 2000 → 3000 for polishing preparation.
Wet or dry
Wet sanding with clean water and light pressure.
Success check
The haze is even, fine, and ready for polishing or the next finish step.

What to do

  1. Keep the surface and paper wet.
  2. Use light pressure.
  3. Refine with the next finer grit.
  4. Wipe and inspect between grits.
  5. Polish after final sanding where a gloss finish is required.
Avoid: Do not expect high gloss immediately after wet sanding alone.
