Scratches Are Too Deep

The surface has visible sanding marks, gouges, or scratches that remain after sanding.

woodmetalpaintclear coatwet sandingdry sanding

Deep scratches remain after coarse grit

Likely cause
The starting grit was too coarse for the finish goal, or the next grit was not fine enough to remove the previous scratch pattern.
Recommended grit
Move one step finer at a time: 80 → 120 → 180 → 220 → 320. For finishing, continue to 400+ as needed.
Wet or dry
Dry for early removal; wet for fine finishing when the surface and project allow it.
Success check
The surface shows one even, finer scratch pattern with no remaining deep lines.

What to do

  1. Stop sanding aggressively in one spot.
  2. Sand evenly with the next finer grit until the old scratch pattern is lighter.
  3. Clean the surface before moving to the next grit.
  4. Repeat with finer grits until the scratches are no longer visible.
Avoid: Do not jump directly from a very coarse grit to a very fine grit.

320 grit does not remove scratches from 180 grit

Likely cause
The grit jump is too large or the 180 grit scratches were too deep from excess pressure.
Recommended grit
Use 220 or 240 grit first, then 320 grit.
Wet or dry
Dry for most prep work; wet only for fine finish stages where appropriate.
Success check
The surface has no visible 180-grit lines before switching back to 320.

What to do

  1. Return to 220 or 240 grit.
  2. Sand with lighter pressure and overlapping strokes.
  3. Clean the surface.
  4. Move to 320 grit only after the 180-grit scratches are gone.
Avoid: Do not press harder with 320 grit to force it to remove deep scratches.
