Finish Looks Uneven

The surface has uneven dull spots, patchy sanding marks, or inconsistent texture.

woodpaintbetween coatshand sandinguneven pressure

Uneven pressure caused patchy sanding

Likely cause
Pressure, stroke direction, or time spent in each area was inconsistent.
Recommended grit
Use the same grit already selected for the stage, then move finer only after the surface is even.
Wet or dry
Dry or wet depending on the stage; keep pressure light and consistent.
Success check
The surface has a consistent scratch pattern and uniform appearance.

What to do

  1. Sand with overlapping strokes.
  2. Keep pressure even.
  3. Use a sanding block on flat surfaces.
  4. Clean and inspect the surface often.
  5. Move to the next grit only after the surface is uniform.
Avoid: Do not focus pressure on one small area for too long.
