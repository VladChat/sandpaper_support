By Grit

Use this section when you already know the grit number or need to choose the next grit.

60–120 — Coarse

Use: Heavy material removal, rough sanding, paint or rust removal, shaping uneven areas.

Avoid: Final finishing or delicate sanding where deep scratches would be a problem.

Next: Move to 150–240 for surface preparation.

150–240 — Medium

Use: Surface preparation, smoothing, sanding before primer, paint, stain, or sealer.

Avoid: Polishing preparation or very fine finish refinement.

Next: Move to 280–400 for finish preparation.

280–400 — Fine

Use: Fine sanding, finish preparation, sanding between suitable coats.

Avoid: Heavy removal tasks.

Next: Move to 600–800 for extra-fine finishing.

600–800 — Extra Fine

Use: Extra-fine finishing, light wet sanding, reducing fine surface marks.

Avoid: Fast cutting or reshaping tasks.

Next: Move to 1000–3000 for polishing preparation.

1000–3000 — Ultra Fine

Use: Very fine wet sanding, polishing preparation, finish refinement.

Avoid: Removing heavy defects or old coatings.

Next: Polish or apply the next finish step as required by the project.
