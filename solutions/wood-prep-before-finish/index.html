Problem

Wood prep before finish

Wood needs a smooth, consistent surface before finish.

wood surface refinement 120 → 150 → 180 → 220 wood prep before finish wood needs a smooth, consistent surface before finish. wood wood needs a smooth, consistent surface before finish how to fix wood prep before finish surface still feels rough
Answer

120 or 150, then 180 and 220. Start with the least aggressive grit that removes roughness.

Why it happens
The prep sequence has not reached an even final scratch pattern.
Recommended grit
120 or 150, then 180 and 220.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The wood feels smooth and looks even before finish is applied.

What to do

  1. Start with the least aggressive grit that removes roughness.
  2. Sand evenly.
  3. Clean dust between grits.
  4. Finish prep at 180 or 220 based on the finish goal.
Avoid: Do not leave coarse scratch marks under finish.
