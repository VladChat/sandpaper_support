Sheet feels smooth but looks unused

The sheet does not look worn out, but it feels smooth and stops cutting.

Solution

Problem
The sheet does not look worn out, but it feels smooth and stops cutting.
Likely cause
Fine dust or finish residue can glaze the abrasive surface before the sheet looks fully used.
Recommended grit
Use a fresh sheet in the same grit, or choose a grit that better matches the task.
Wet or dry
Dry or wet depending on the stage.
Success check
The surface looks and feels even before the next step.

Steps

  1. Clean the surface and the sheet.
  2. Use clean water and light pressure.
  3. Refine with the recommended grit sequence.
  4. Wipe and inspect before moving to the next step.
Avoid: Do not let dirty slurry or trapped grit stay under the sheet.
