Problem

Which grit is best before painting?

The user needs a grit for paint prep.

paint primer dry sanding between coats
Answer

Use 180 to 220 before painting. Use 320 to 400 for primer or between-coat prep.

Why it happens
Paint needs a clean, even surface with enough tooth for the coating.
Recommended grit
Use 180 to 220 for general paint prep. Use 320 to 400 for primer and between-coat prep.
Wet or dry
Dry for paint prep.
Success check
The surface is clean, dull, and evenly sanded before paint.

What to do

  1. Remove loose material first.
  2. Sand rough areas with 180.
  3. Use 220 for general paint prep.
  4. Use 320 or 400 for primer or between-coat prep.
Avoid: Do not paint over dust, gloss, or loose coating.
