Problem

320 grit does not remove 180 grit scratches

Scratches from 180 grit remain after sanding with 320 grit.

general sandpaper use scratch removal 180 → 220 → 240 → 320 320 grit does not remove 180 grit scratches scratches from 180 grit remain after sanding with 320 grit. general sandpaper use scratches from 180 grit remain after sanding with 320 grit how to fix 320 grit does not remove 180 grit scratches scratches too deep
Answer

220 or 240 before returning to 320. Return to 220 or 240 grit.

Why it happens
The jump from 180 to 320 is too large for the depth of the scratches.
Recommended grit
220 or 240 before returning to 320.
Wet or dry
Dry for normal prep work.
Success check
No visible 180-grit lines remain before 320 grit.

What to do

  1. Return to 220 or 240 grit.
  2. Use light, even pressure.
  3. Clean the dust before switching grits.
  4. Use 320 only after the deeper lines are gone.
Avoid: Do not press harder with 320 grit to force removal.
