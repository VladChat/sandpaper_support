Problem

What grit should I use for automotive wet sanding?

The user needs a safe fine-grit path for automotive wet sanding.

clear coat wet sanding dry sanding between coats
Answer

For automotive wet sanding, use 1000, 1500, 2000, then 3000 with clean water and light pressure.

Why it happens
Automotive finish work usually needs gradual fine-grit refinement and clean water.
Recommended grit
Use 1000, 1500, 2000, then 3000 for fine wet sanding stages.
Wet or dry
Wet.
Success check
The surface has a uniform fine haze with no isolated deep scratches.

What to do

  1. Wash and rinse the surface first.
  2. Start with the finest grit that still corrects the issue.
  3. Use clean water and light pressure.
  4. Step finer only after the previous marks are even.
Avoid: Do not use coarse grit on clear coat without a clear reason and experience.
