Problem

What grit to level high-build primer?

High-build primer can have texture, waves, or guide-coat marks that need leveling.

paint primer wet sanding dry sanding
Answer

For high-build primer, level with 320, then refine with 400 or 600 before paint.

Why it happens
High-build primer is designed to be sanded, but it must be leveled with a grit that cuts without leaving deep final scratches.
Recommended grit
Use 320 for leveling, then 400 or 600 before paint.
Wet or dry
Dry for leveling; wet at finer stages only when the coating system allows it.
Success check
The primer is level, smooth, and has a uniform scratch pattern before paint.

What to do

  1. Let primer cure as directed.
  2. Block sand with 320 to level texture.
  3. Refine with 400 or 600.
  4. Clean thoroughly before paint.
Avoid: Do not paint over unlevel primer texture.
