Problem

Scratches remain on metal after prep

Metal shows straight or uneven sanding scratches.

Answer

Use 180, 220, 320, then 400 or 600 if a finer prep is needed. Remove residue from the metal.

Why it happens
The metal was not refined through enough intermediate grits.
Recommended grit
180, 220, 320, then 400 or 600 if a finer prep is needed.
Wet or dry
Dry for shaping; wet can help at finer stages where suitable.
Success check
The old scratch direction is no longer visible.

What to do

  1. Remove residue from the metal.
  2. Sand with the next finer grit until the old pattern is uniform.
  3. Change direction slightly between grits to see progress.
  4. Clean before each grit change.
Avoid: Do not leave loose grit or debris on the surface.

Ask a follow-up

Ask about this answer, the next grit, or wet/dry use.
