Problem

Wet sanding metal finish

Metal finish sanding needs a controlled wet sanding sequence to reduce haze and deep marks.

metal wet sanding refinement 600 → 800 → 1000 → 1500 wet sanding metal metal wet sanding haze metal finish grit sequence how to wet sand metal wet sanding steel prep
Answer

Use 600 grit to start wet sanding when the metal is evenly prepped, then progress to 800, 1000, and 1500 as needed.

Why it happens
Wet sanding haze stays when progression is rushed or water and residue are not managed between steps.
Recommended grit
600, then 800, 1000, and 1500 depending on finish target.
Wet or dry
Use dry sanding before wet sanding. Use wet sanding from 600 and finer with clean water.
Success check
The metal surface shows a uniform fine pattern without random deep lines.

What to do

  1. Dry-prep the metal until the surface is even.
  2. Start wet sanding at 600 grit with clean water.
  3. Progress to 800 and 1000 grit after each pattern is uniform.
  4. Use 1500 grit if a finer final prep is needed.
  5. Rinse slurry often to avoid dragging particles.
  6. Dry and inspect under direct light before finishing.
Avoid: Do not wet sand with dirty water or skip cleanup between grit steps.
