Problem

Removing old paint from metal

Old paint on metal is slow to remove and leaves uneven residue.

Answer

Start with 80 grit for heavy paint removal, then use 120 and 180 grit to clean and level before finer prep.

Why it happens
Removal is slow when the starting grit is too fine for the coating thickness.
Recommended grit
80 for removal, then 120 and 180; use 220 for prep before coating.
Wet or dry
Use dry sanding for paint removal. Use wet sanding only at finer prep stages if needed.
Success check
Old paint is removed and the metal shows an even prep pattern before coating.

What to do

  1. Test a small area with 80 grit for removal speed.
  2. Use 60 grit only on very thick or hard coating spots.
  3. Switch to 120 grit when most old paint is removed.
  4. Use 180 then 220 grit to refine the metal prep pattern.
  5. Clean dust and residue between grit changes.
  6. Inspect for remaining paint islands before primer.
Avoid: Do not use very fine grit first for heavy old paint removal.
