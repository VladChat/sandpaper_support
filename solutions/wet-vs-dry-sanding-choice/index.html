Problem

Wet vs dry sanding

You need to choose whether to sand wet or dry for the current stage.

general sandpaper use grit selection 120 → 320 → 600 → 1000 wet vs dry sanding should i wet sand or dry sand when to wet sand dry sanding vs wet sanding sanding method choice
Answer

Use dry sanding for shaping and early defect removal. Use wet sanding for finer refinement from 600 grit and above when the surface can be rinsed safely.

Why it happens
Confusion comes from mixing removal and finishing stages without a clear method plan.
Recommended grit
Dry stage: 120 to 320. Wet stage: 600 to 1000 and finer.
Wet or dry
Dry for early stages and material removal. Wet for finer stages and haze reduction.
Success check
Method choice matches the stage and the surface refines predictably.

What to do

  1. Use dry sanding for rough shaping and defect removal.
  2. Confirm the surface is even before switching methods.
  3. Start wet sanding at 600 grit or finer when suitable.
  4. Use clean water and rinse residue often.
  5. Progress through finer grits only after each pattern is uniform.
  6. Dry and inspect before final coating or polish.
Avoid: Do not start wet sanding too early or use dirty slurry between grit steps.
