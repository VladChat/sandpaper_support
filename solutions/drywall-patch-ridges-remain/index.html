Problem

Drywall patch ridges remain

Drywall patch ridges are still visible or feel high after sanding.

drywall surface refinement 120 → 180 → 220 drywall patch ridges remain drywall patch ridges are still visible or feel high after sanding. drywall drywall patch ridges are still visible or feel high after sanding how to fix drywall patch ridges remain surface still feels rough
Answer

Use 120 to 180 for leveling, then 220 for light smoothing. Let the patch dry fully.

Why it happens
The ridges were not leveled before switching to a finer finishing grit.
Recommended grit
Use 120 to 180 for leveling, then 220 for light smoothing.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The surface looks and feels even before the next step.

What to do

  1. Let the patch dry fully.
  2. Sand the high area with light pressure.
  3. Feather the edge into the surrounding surface.
  4. Remove dust before painting.
Avoid: Do not press hard enough to tear paper or create low spots.
