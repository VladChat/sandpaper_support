Problem

Grit is too coarse between coats

Sanding between coats leaves scratches or cuts through coating.

paint / primer between-coats prep 320 → 600 grit is too coarse between coats sanding between coats leaves scratches or cuts through coating. paint / primer sanding between coats leaves scratches or cuts through coating how to fix grit is too coarse between coats poor results between coats
Answer

320 to 600 depending on coating stage. Confirm the coating is ready to sand.

Why it happens
The grit is intended for removal instead of light intercoat prep.
Recommended grit
320 to 600 depending on coating stage.
Wet or dry
Light dry sanding or wet sanding where suitable.
Success check
The coating is lightly dulled and smooth without deep scratches.

What to do

  1. Confirm the coating is ready to sand.
  2. Use light pressure.
  3. Smooth only the surface defects.
  4. Clean completely before recoating.
Avoid: Do not use 80, 120, or 180 grit for fine intercoat sanding.
