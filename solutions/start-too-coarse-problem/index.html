Problem

What happens if I start too coarse?

The user started with coarse grit and now has deep scratches.

wet sanding dry sanding scratches
Answer

Coarse grit leaves deep scratches. Refine step by step through medium and fine grits.

Why it happens
Coarse grit removes material quickly and leaves a deep scratch pattern.
Recommended grit
Refine through the next grits in order until the coarse scratches are removed.
Wet or dry
Dry for early refinement; wet for fine finishing stages.
Success check
The deep coarse pattern is replaced by a finer even pattern.

What to do

  1. Stop using the coarse grit once material removal is done.
  2. Move to the next medium grit.
  3. Inspect for remaining deep scratches.
  4. Continue finer step by step.
Avoid: Do not try to hide coarse scratches with only ultra-fine grit.
