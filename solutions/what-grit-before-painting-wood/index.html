Problem

What grit before painting wood?

You need the right grit sequence before painting a wood surface.

Quick Answer

Use 120 grit for leveling, then 150 and 180 for prep. Use 220 as a final step when the wood needs a smoother paint-ready finish.

Best Grit Path

100 only if very uneven → 120 → 150 → 180 → 220

Step-by-Step Fix
  1. Remove dust and debris from the wood.
  2. Start with 120 grit for leveling.
  3. Move to 150 grit to refine the pattern.
  4. Use 180 grit for paint prep baseline.
  5. Use 220 grit for smoother final prep when needed.
  6. Wipe clean before primer or paint.
Why It Happens

Paint shows defects when wood prep skips intermediate grits or leaves coarse lines.

Wet or Dry

Use dry sanding for wood prep before primer or paint.

Mistakes to Avoid
  • Do not skip from 120 directly to 220.
  • Do not leave end-grain rougher than flat areas.
  • Do not coat before cleaning the surface.
Success Check

The wood feels smooth and has an even prep pattern without coarse scratch lines.
