Problem

How to move through grits correctly

Grit progression is unclear and scratches keep carrying into finer steps.

Quick Answer

Use small grit jumps and fully remove each scratch pattern before moving finer. A common path is 120, 180, 220, 320, then 400 or finer as needed.

Best Grit Path

80 only if very uneven → 120 → 180 → 220 → 320 → 400

Step-by-Step Fix
  1. Pick the coarsest grit needed for the current defect.
  2. Sand until the scratch pattern is even at that grit.
  3. Move to the next finer grit with a moderate jump.
  4. Inspect under good light before each transition.
  5. Repeat until the target finish stage is reached.
  6. Clean residue between every grit step.
Why It Happens

Scratch transfer happens when grit jumps are too large or step completion is inconsistent.

Wet or Dry

Dry sanding for early steps, wet sanding at fine stages when appropriate.

Mistakes to Avoid
  • Do not move on before the current step is fully even.
  • Do not use large grit jumps on visible-finish surfaces.
  • Do not ignore lighting checks between stages.
Success Check

Each finer step removes the previous pattern without leftover deep lines.
