Problem

What grit before painting drywall?

Drywall and patch areas need the right grit sequence before primer and paint.

drywall grit selection 120 → 150 → 180 → 220 what grit before painting drywall drywall prep grit sequence drywall patch sanding grit grit for drywall before primer drywall edge still visible
Answer

Use 120 grit for shaping patch ridges, then 150 and 180 for blending. Finish with 220 grit when smoother final prep is needed.

Why it happens
Visible patch edges usually come from uneven shaping or finishing too coarse before paint.
Recommended grit
120, then 150 and 180. Use 220 for smoother final prep.
Wet or dry
Use dry sanding for drywall prep.
Success check
The patch area blends into surrounding drywall with no visible ridge under light.

What to do

  1. Check patch edges and high spots first.
  2. Use 120 grit for shaping ridges and transitions.
  3. Move to 150 grit for more even blending.
  4. Use 180 grit to refine the prep pattern.
  5. Finish with 220 grit when the painted finish requires smoother prep.
  6. Remove dust before primer and paint.
Avoid: Do not leave coarse scratch lines or visible edge transitions before coating.
