Problem

Fine grit used for removal

Sanding takes a long time and little material is removed.

general sandpaper use material removal 60 → 120 fine grit used for removal sanding takes a long time and little material is removed. general sandpaper use sanding takes a long time and little material is removed how to fix fine grit used for removal sanding takes too long
Answer

60 to 120 for heavy removal, then step finer. Switch to a grit that actually cuts the defect.

Why it happens
Fine grit refines scratches but is not efficient for heavy removal.
Recommended grit
60 to 120 for heavy removal, then step finer.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The defect reduces visibly in a reasonable number of strokes.

What to do

  1. Switch to a grit that actually cuts the defect.
  2. Sand evenly.
  3. Move to intermediate grits.
  4. Finish with finer grits only after leveling.
Avoid: Do not keep rubbing with a fine grit when the defect is still high or deep.
