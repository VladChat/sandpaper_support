Problem

What grit between polyurethane coats?

Polyurethane coats can feel rough or show small dust nibs between coats.

wood wet sanding dry sanding between coats
Answer

Between cured polyurethane coats, scuff lightly with 320 or 400 and remove all dust.

Why it happens
Light scuff sanding helps level dust nibs and gives the next coat a clean surface.
Recommended grit
Use 320 or 400 between cured polyurethane coats.
Wet or dry
Dry; wet only when the finish instructions allow it.
Success check
The coat feels smooth and dull without sanding through the finish.

What to do

  1. Let the polyurethane cure as directed.
  2. Scuff lightly with 320 or 400.
  3. Avoid sanding through edges.
  4. Remove dust completely before the next coat.
Avoid: Do not sand uncured polyurethane.
