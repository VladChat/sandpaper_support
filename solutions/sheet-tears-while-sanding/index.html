Problem

Sheet tears while sanding

The sandpaper sheet tears during use.

wet sanding dry sanding scratches pressure
Answer

Reduce pressure, avoid sharp snags, and use backing support. Replace torn pieces before finish sanding.

Why it happens
Sharp edges, high pressure, repeated folding, or snagging can tear a sheet.
Recommended grit
Use the grit required for the task and reduce pressure or use backing support.
Wet or dry
Dry for normal prep; wet for fine wet-sanding stages.
Success check
The sheet moves smoothly without snagging or leaving torn-edge marks.

What to do

  1. Check for sharp corners or burrs.
  2. Use a sanding block for support.
  3. Fold the sheet cleanly instead of crumpling it.
  4. Replace torn pieces that can scratch the surface.
Avoid: Do not keep using torn edges on fine finish work.
