Problem

End grain sands differently

End grain looks or feels different from the rest of the wood after sanding.

Quick Answer

Use the same final prep grit, often 180 or 220, with careful light pressure. Sand with the grain where possible.

Best Grit Path

180 → 220

Step-by-Step Fix
  1. Sand with the grain where possible.
  2. Use the grit that removes the visible issue.
  3. Step finer gradually.
  4. Remove dust and inspect under good light.
Why It Happens

End grain absorbs and abrades differently, so pressure and grit choice show more.

Wet or Dry

Dry.

Mistakes to Avoid
  • Do not skip grit steps while visible scratches or high spots remain.
Success Check

The surface looks and feels even before the next step.

Related Problems
  • Uneven finish from pressure
  • Finger grooves from hand sanding
  • Patchy surface after grit change
