End grain sands differently

End grain looks or feels different from the rest of the wood after sanding.

Solution

Problem
End grain looks or feels different from the rest of the wood after sanding.
Likely cause
End grain absorbs and abrades differently, so pressure and grit choice show more.
Recommended grit
Use the same final prep grit, often 180 or 220, with careful light pressure.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The surface looks and feels even before the next step.

Steps

  1. Sand with the grain where possible.
  2. Use the grit that removes the visible issue.
  3. Step finer gradually.
  4. Remove dust and inspect under good light.
Avoid: Do not skip grit steps while visible scratches or high spots remain.
