Problem

What grit for teak before oil?

Teak can look patchy or dull if the surface is not prepared evenly before oil.

wood drywall patch dry sanding haze
Answer

For teak before oil, use 180 then 220. Start with 120 only on weathered or rough areas.

Why it happens
Teak is dense and oily, so sanding dust and uneven scratch patterns can affect the final look.
Recommended grit
Use 180 then 220 before oil. For refinishing weathered teak, start with 120 only where needed.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The teak has an even color and smooth feel before oiling.

What to do

  1. Clean the teak surface first.
  2. Use 120 only on weathered or rough areas.
  3. Sand through 180 and 220.
  4. Remove dust thoroughly before applying oil.
Avoid: Do not oil over sanding dust or grey oxidized wood.
