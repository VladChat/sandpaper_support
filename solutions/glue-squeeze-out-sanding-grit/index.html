Problem

What grit removes dried wood glue?

Dried glue squeeze-out can leave raised spots and block stain absorption.

wood dry sanding clogging medium grit
Answer

For dried wood glue, scrape first, then sand with 180 and blend with 220.

Why it happens
Wood glue must be removed cleanly because leftover glue can seal the wood surface.
Recommended grit
Scrape first, then use 180 and 220 to blend the area.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The glue is gone and the wood surface absorbs finish evenly.

What to do

  1. Scrape dried glue carefully first.
  2. Sand the remaining glue spot with 180.
  3. Blend with 220.
  4. Inspect before stain or finish.
Avoid: Do not stain over glue residue.
