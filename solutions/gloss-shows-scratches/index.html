Problem

Gloss shows sanding scratches

Scratches that were hard to see become obvious on a glossy surface.

Answer

Refine through finer grits; for clear coat prep use 1500, 2000, and 3000 before polishing. Clean the surface and the sheet.

Why it happens
Gloss reflects light strongly and reveals scratches that were not fully refined.
Recommended grit
Refine through finer grits; for clear coat prep use 1500, 2000, and 3000 before polishing.
Wet or dry
Wet for fine clear coat or gloss prep; dry for earlier prep.
Success check
The old marks are replaced by one uniform finer scratch pattern.

What to do

  1. Clean the surface and the sheet.
  2. Use clean water and light pressure.
  3. Refine with the recommended grit sequence.
  4. Wipe and inspect before moving to the next step.
Avoid: Do not expect sanding alone to create full gloss.
