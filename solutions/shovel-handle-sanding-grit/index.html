Problem

What grit for sanding a shovel handle?

A shovel handle can feel rough, dirty, or splintery.

wood dry sanding coarse grit medium grit
Answer

For a shovel handle, use 120 on splinters, then 180 and 220 before oil or sealant.

Why it happens
Wood handles need practical smoothing without removing too much material.
Recommended grit
Use 120 for splinters, then 180 and 220 before oil or sealant.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The handle feels smooth and safe to hold.

What to do

  1. Clean the handle.
  2. Sand splintery areas with 120.
  3. Refine with 180.
  4. Finish with 220 before oil or sealant.
Avoid: Do not leave sharp splinters in the grip area.
