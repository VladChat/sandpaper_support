Problem

What grit to scuff oil-based paint?

New paint may not grip old oil-based paint well without surface prep.

paint primer dry sanding polishing prep
Answer

To scuff oil-based paint, clean first, then use 180 or 220. Use 320 for smoother visible areas.

Why it happens
Old oil-based paint can be glossy and slick, so it needs cleaning and scuff sanding before repainting.
Recommended grit
Use 180 or 220 for scuff sanding, then 320 if a smoother surface is needed.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The old paint is clean, dull, and evenly scuffed.

What to do

  1. Clean the painted surface first.
  2. Scuff glossy paint with 180 or 220.
  3. Smooth visible areas with 320 if needed.
  4. Remove dust before primer or paint.
Avoid: Do not repaint glossy oil-based paint without proper prep.
