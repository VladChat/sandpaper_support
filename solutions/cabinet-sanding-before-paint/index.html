Problem

Cabinet sanding before paint

Cabinet doors and frames need even sanding prep before paint.

Quick Answer

Use 120 grit for leveling, then 150 and 180 for prep. Finish with 220 grit for smoother paint-ready cabinets.

Best Grit Path

100 only if very uneven → 120 → 150 → 180 → 220

Step-by-Step Fix
  1. Clean cabinet faces, edges, and profiles.
  2. Level with 120 grit where needed.
  3. Refine with 150 then 180 grit across all surfaces.
  4. Use 220 grit for final prep before primer.
  5. Feather sharp profile transitions with light pressure.
  6. Remove dust thoroughly before coating.
Why It Happens

Cabinet paint defects happen when coarse scratches or edge burn-through remain before coating.

Wet or Dry

Use dry sanding for cabinet prep.

Mistakes to Avoid
  • Do not over-sand corners and profile ridges.
  • Do not skip from coarse grit to final prep grit.
  • Do not prime over leftover sanding dust.
Success Check

Cabinet surfaces are smooth, clean, and consistent before primer and paint.
