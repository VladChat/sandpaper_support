Problem

What grit for metal table legs before painting?

Paint on metal table legs can chip or peel if the surface is too slick.

metal paint primer dry sanding
Answer

For metal table legs, clean first, scuff with 220 or 320, then refine with 400.

Why it happens
Metal legs often have glossy paint, oil, or light rust that must be cleaned and scuffed.
Recommended grit
Use 220 or 320 for scuffing, then 400 for smoother visible prep.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The legs are clean, dull, and ready for primer or paint.

What to do

  1. Clean grease and residue first.
  2. Remove rust if present.
  3. Scuff with 220 or 320.
  4. Use 400 for smoother visible areas.
Avoid: Do not paint over oil, dust, or glossy metal.
