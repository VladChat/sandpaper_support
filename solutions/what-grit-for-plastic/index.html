Problem

What grit should I use for plastic?

You need a safe grit sequence for sanding plastic without deep scratches.

Quick Answer

Start with 400 grit for general plastic prep. Move to 600, 800, and 1000 for smoother refinement. Use 320 only when defects are deeper.

Best Grit Path

320 only if very uneven → 400 → 600 → 800 → 1000

Step-by-Step Fix
  1. Clean the plastic surface.
  2. Start with 400 grit for standard prep.
  3. Use 320 only on isolated deeper marks.
  4. Move to 600 grit after the scratch pattern is even.
  5. Finish with 800 then 1000 grit for final refinement.
  6. Rinse or wipe residue before each grit change.
Why It Happens

Plastic damage happens when the starting grit is too coarse or grit steps are skipped.

Wet or Dry

Dry sand early shaping. Use wet sanding from 600 and finer when plastic can be safely rinsed.

Mistakes to Avoid
  • Do not start with very coarse grit unless defects are clearly deep.
  • Do not jump directly from 320 to 1000 grit.
  • Do not move to the next grit until the current scratch pattern is even.
Success Check

The plastic feels even and the scratch pattern is fine and consistent.

Related Problems
  • Plastic still feels rough
  • Plastic haze after wet sanding
  • Plastic melts or smears
  • Skipped from 120 to 320
