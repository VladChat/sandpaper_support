Problem

Can I sand Venetian plaster?

Venetian plaster can lose sheen or show dull spots if sanded too aggressively.

drywall patch dry sanding haze pressure
Answer

For Venetian plaster, avoid coarse sanding. Test 600 or finer lightly in a hidden area.

Why it happens
Decorative plaster finishes rely on burnishing and surface texture, so sanding can change the look.
Recommended grit
Avoid coarse sanding. For small corrections, test 600 or finer very lightly.
Wet or dry
Dry unless the finish maker specifies otherwise.
Success check
The correction does not create a visible dull patch.

What to do

  1. Identify whether the finish is sealed or unsealed.
  2. Test a hidden spot first.
  3. Use 600 or finer only for small corrections.
  4. Stop if sheen or color changes unevenly.
Avoid: Do not use coarse grit on decorative plaster finish.
