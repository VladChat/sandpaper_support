Problem

What grit removes epoxy drips on wood?

Epoxy drips can leave raised hard spots on a wood surface.

wood wet sanding dry sanding pressure
Answer

For cured epoxy drips on wood, level with 120 or 180, then blend with 220 and 320.

Why it happens
Cured epoxy is harder than surrounding wood, so it needs leveling without digging into the wood.
Recommended grit
Use 120 or 180 to level cured drips, then 220 and 320 to blend.
Wet or dry
Dry for leveling; wet only for final epoxy finishing where suitable.
Success check
The drip is flush and the surrounding wood is not gouged.

What to do

  1. Let epoxy cure fully.
  2. Level raised drips with 120 or 180.
  3. Blend with 220.
  4. Refine with 320 before finish.
Avoid: Do not sand uncured sticky epoxy.
