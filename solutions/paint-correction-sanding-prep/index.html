Problem

Paint correction sanding prep

Correction prep for painted surfaces needs controlled high-grit refinement before polishing.

Quick Answer

Use 1000 grit only when defects need leveling, then move through 1500, 2000, and 3000 before polishing.

Best Grit Path

800 only if very uneven → 1000 → 1500 → 2000 → 3000

Step-by-Step Fix
  1. Confirm coating thickness and defect depth before sanding.
  2. Start at 1000 grit only when defect leveling is needed.
  3. Progress to 1500 and 2000 once the pattern is even.
  4. Use 3000 grit for final refinement before polishing.
  5. Rinse and inspect after each step under clear light.
  6. Polish only after a uniform fine haze is achieved.
Why It Happens

Correction prep fails when defects are not fully refined before polishing.

Wet or Dry

Use wet sanding for the high-grit correction sequence with clean water and controlled pressure.

Mistakes to Avoid
  • Do not start too coarse when defects are shallow.
  • Do not continue sanding edges aggressively.
  • Do not skip intermediate grit refinement before polishing.
Success Check

The surface shows a uniform fine haze that is ready for predictable polishing.
