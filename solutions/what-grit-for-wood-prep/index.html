Problem

What grit for wood prep

The wood needs to be prepared but the grit is unclear.

wood grit selection 120 → 150 → 180 → 220 what grit for wood prep the wood needs to be prepared but the grit is unclear. wood wood needs to be prepared but the grit is unclear how to fix what grit for wood prep not sure what grit to use
Answer

120 or 150 for rough prep, then 180 or 220 for smoother prep. Start only as coarse as needed.

Why it happens
Wood prep depends on how rough the surface is before sanding.
Recommended grit
120 or 150 for rough prep, then 180 or 220 for smoother prep.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The wood feels smooth and shows an even scratch pattern.

What to do

  1. Start only as coarse as needed.
  2. Sand evenly with the grain when possible.
  3. Move to 180 or 220.
  4. Remove dust before finishing.
Avoid: Do not over-sand soft areas with heavy pressure.
