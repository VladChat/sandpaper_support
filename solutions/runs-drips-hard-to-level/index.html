Problem

Runs or drips are hard to level

Paint or clear coat runs remain raised after sanding.

automotive / clear coat surface refinement 800 → 1500 runs or drips are hard to level paint or clear coat runs remain raised after sanding. automotive / clear coat paint or clear coat runs remain raised after sanding how to fix runs or drips are hard to level surface still feels rough
Answer

Use 800 to 1500 depending on the coating and defect height, then refine finer. Clean the surface and the sheet.

Why it happens
A raised run needs careful leveling before fine refinement.
Recommended grit
Use 800 to 1500 depending on the coating and defect height, then refine finer.
Wet or dry
Wet for fine coating work.
Success check
The surface looks and feels even before the next step.

What to do

  1. Clean the surface and the sheet.
  2. Use clean water and light pressure.
  3. Refine with the recommended grit sequence.
  4. Wipe and inspect before moving to the next step.
Avoid: Do not let dirty slurry or trapped grit stay under the sheet.
