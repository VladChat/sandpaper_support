Problem

What grit for wooden toys?

Wooden toys need smooth edges without over-rounding details.

wood dry sanding scratches pressure
Answer

For wooden toys, use 180 for cleanup, then 220 and 320 for a smooth hand feel.

Why it happens
Coarse sanding can leave scratches, while heavy pressure can change the shape of small parts.
Recommended grit
Use 180 for shaping cleanup, then 220 and 320 for smooth hand feel.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The toy feels smooth by hand with no sharp splinters or deep scratches.

What to do

  1. Remove sharp edges lightly with 180 grit.
  2. Smooth the surface with 220.
  3. Use 320 for final touch areas.
  4. Remove all dust before applying a safe finish.
Avoid: Do not use coarse grit aggressively on small toy parts.
