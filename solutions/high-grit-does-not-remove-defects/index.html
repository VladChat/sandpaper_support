Problem

High grit does not remove defects

Fine grit does not remove scratches, chips, or raised defects.

general sandpaper use material removal 180 → 400 high grit does not remove defects fine grit does not remove scratches, chips, or raised defects. general sandpaper use fine grit does not remove scratches, chips, or raised defects how to fix high grit does not remove defects sanding takes too long
Answer

Return to the least aggressive grit that actually cuts the defect, often 180 to 400 depending on severity. Clean the surface and the sheet.

Why it happens
High grits refine a surface but do not remove deeper defects efficiently.
Recommended grit
Return to the least aggressive grit that actually cuts the defect, often 180 to 400 depending on severity.
Wet or dry
Dry or wet depending on the surface and stage.
Success check
The old marks are replaced by one uniform finer scratch pattern.

What to do

  1. Clean the surface and the sheet.
  2. Use clean water and light pressure.
  3. Refine with the recommended grit sequence.
  4. Wipe and inspect before moving to the next step.
Avoid: Do not let dirty slurry or trapped grit stay under the sheet.
