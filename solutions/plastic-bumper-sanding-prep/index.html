Problem

Plastic bumper sanding before paint

A plastic bumper needs smooth sanding prep before primer or paint.

Quick Answer

Use 400 grit to level, then 600 and 800 grit for prep. Finish with 1000 grit when a finer paint-ready surface is needed.

Best Grit Path

320 only if very uneven → 400 → 600 → 800 → 1000

Step-by-Step Fix
  1. Wash and dry the bumper before sanding.
  2. Start with 400 grit for general leveling.
  3. Use 320 grit only on heavier texture or defects.
  4. Move to 600 grit once the surface is even.
  5. Refine with 800 grit and finish with 1000 as needed.
  6. Clean dust and residue before primer or paint.
Why It Happens

Bumpers stay rough when sanding pressure is uneven or grit progression is too aggressive.

Wet or Dry

Dry sand for leveling. Wet sand from 600 and finer if the bumper material and repair products allow water.

Mistakes to Avoid
  • Do not press hard on curved edges.
  • Do not skip directly from coarse sanding to final paint prep grit.
  • Do not leave sanding dust before coating.
Success Check

The bumper feels smooth and shows an even fine prep pattern before coating.
