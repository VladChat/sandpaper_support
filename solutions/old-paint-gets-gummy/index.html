Old paint gets gummy

Old paint softens or gums up the sandpaper.

Solution

Problem
Old paint softens or gums up the sandpaper.
Likely cause
The coating is smearing from heat, pressure, or softness instead of sanding to dust.
Recommended grit
Use the removal or prep grit needed, often 80 to 220, with fresh paper.
Wet or dry
Dry for removal; wet only for suitable fine stages.
Success check
The abrasive cuts cleanly without packed residue.

Steps

  1. Clean the surface and the sheet.
  2. Use clean water and light pressure.
  3. Refine with the recommended grit sequence.
  4. Wipe and inspect before moving to the next step.
Avoid: Do not let dirty slurry or trapped grit stay under the sheet.
