Problem

What grit for barnwood?

Barnwood can be splintery, dirty, or too rough for finishing.

wood metal dry sanding coarse grit
Answer

For barnwood, use 80 or 100 on splinters, then 120 or 150 for a smoother rustic finish.

Why it happens
Reclaimed wood needs cleaning and controlled sanding so character is not removed accidentally.
Recommended grit
Use 80 or 100 for splinters, then 120 or 150 for a smoother rustic finish.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The wood is safer to handle but still keeps the intended rustic character.

What to do

  1. Remove dirt, loose debris, and hidden metal first.
  2. Sand splintery areas with 80 or 100.
  3. Refine with 120 or 150.
  4. Stop before removing the desired aged surface.
Avoid: Do not sand away the aged texture unless that is the goal.
