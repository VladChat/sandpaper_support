Problem

How do I remove gloss before repainting?

The painted surface is glossy and needs prep before repainting.

paint primer dry sanding polishing prep
Answer

Clean first, then scuff with 220. Use 320 or 400 for finer prep.

Why it happens
New paint usually needs a clean, dull, lightly abraded surface for prep.
Recommended grit
Use 220 for general scuffing; use 320 or 400 for finer prep.
Wet or dry
Dry for general prep.
Success check
The surface is evenly dull and clean.

What to do

  1. Clean the glossy surface first.
  2. Scuff evenly with 220 grit.
  3. Use 320 or 400 for lighter fine prep.
  4. Remove dust before repainting.
Avoid: Do not repaint over a dusty or glossy unprepared surface.
