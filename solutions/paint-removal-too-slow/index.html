Problem

Paint removal is too slow

Paint is not coming off efficiently.

Quick Answer

60, 80, or 120 for removal depending on the surface and finish goal. Use a coarser grit suited to removal.

Best Grit Path

60 → 80 → 120

Step-by-Step Fix
  1. Use a coarser grit suited to removal.
  2. Clear dust and paint residue often.
  3. Replace worn paper.
  4. Refine scratches with finer grits afterward.
Why It Happens

The grit may be too fine, loaded, or worn for removal.

Wet or Dry

Dry for removal.

Mistakes to Avoid
  • Do not remove paint with 400+ grit unless only light scuffing is needed.
Success Check

Paint removal speeds up without excessive pressure.

Related Problems
  • Fine grit used for removal
  • Worn sheet is not cutting
  • Sheet feels smooth but looks unused
