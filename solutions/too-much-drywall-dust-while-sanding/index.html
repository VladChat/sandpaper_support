Problem

Too much drywall dust while sanding

Drywall sanding creates excessive dust and slows progress.

Answer

Use 150 or 180 grit for controlled drywall refinement and clean dust often. Switch sheets when loading reduces cutting.

Why it happens
Dust buildup and loaded abrasive reduce cutting and spread residue across the patch.
Recommended grit
150 or 180 for blending, then 220 for final refinement.
Wet or dry
Use dry sanding with frequent dust cleanup for drywall work.
Success check
Dust remains manageable and the surface blends evenly without smeared residue.

What to do

  1. Use 150 or 180 grit for primary blending.
  2. Sand with light pressure and overlapping strokes.
  3. Wipe or vacuum dust regularly to keep cutting action.
  4. Switch to 220 grit for final smoothing.
  5. Replace loaded sheets before they glaze over.
  6. Clean the area before primer.
Avoid: Do not keep sanding with loaded paper that only smears dust.
