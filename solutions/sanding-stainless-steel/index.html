Problem

Sanding stainless steel

Stainless steel stays rough or scratched after sanding prep.

Answer

Start with 180 grit for leveling, then move through 220, 320, and 400 grit to refine stainless steel.

Why it happens
Stainless steel defects persist when coarse marks are not fully removed before moving finer.
Recommended grit
180, then 220, 320, and 400 for smoother prep.
Wet or dry
Use dry sanding for early correction. Wet sanding can help from 320 and finer when cleanup control is needed.
Success check
The stainless surface has an even scratch pattern at the final prep grit.

What to do

  1. Clean the stainless surface before sanding.
  2. Use 180 grit to remove visible defects.
  3. Move to 220 grit once 180 marks are even.
  4. Use 320 then 400 grit to refine the surface.
  5. Sand in a consistent direction for easier scratch tracking.
  6. Clean residue before coating or finishing.
Avoid: Do not jump from coarse grit directly to very fine grit on stainless steel.
