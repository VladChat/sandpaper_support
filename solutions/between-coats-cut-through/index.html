Problem

Sanding cuts through between coats

Sanding removes too much coating in some areas.

Answer

Use a finer grit such as 400 or 600 for delicate intercoat smoothing. Reduce pressure.

Why it happens
Pressure is too high, grit is too coarse, or edges are being overworked.
Recommended grit
Use a finer grit such as 400 or 600 for delicate intercoat smoothing.
Wet or dry
Light dry sanding or wet sanding if appropriate.
Success check
The surface is smooth without exposing lower layers.

What to do

  1. Reduce pressure.
  2. Use a finer grit.
  3. Avoid dwelling on edges.
  4. Clean before deciding on the next coat.
Avoid: Do not try to level thick defects with fine intercoat sanding.
