Problem

Joint compound clogs paper

Joint compound dust packs into the sandpaper quickly.

Answer

Use 120 to 220 depending on patch height and finish stage. Let the patch dry fully.

Why it happens
Fine compound dust can load the abrasive if it is not cleared often.
Recommended grit
Use 120 to 220 depending on patch height and finish stage.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The abrasive cuts cleanly without packed residue.

What to do

  1. Let the patch dry fully.
  2. Sand the high area with light pressure.
  3. Feather the edge into the surrounding surface.
  4. Remove dust before painting.
Avoid: Do not compensate for loading or wear with heavy pressure.
