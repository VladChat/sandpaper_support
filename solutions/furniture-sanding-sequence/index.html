Problem

Furniture sanding sequence

Furniture sanding needs a controlled grit progression for even finish prep.

wood grit selection 120 → 150 → 180 → 220 furniture sanding sequence how to sand furniture furniture grit progression wood furniture prep before finish furniture feels rough after sanding
Answer

Start with 120 grit, then use 150 and 180. Finish with 220 before stain or paint when a smoother result is required.

Why it happens
Furniture surfaces show defects when grit steps are skipped or pressure is uneven on edges and details.
Recommended grit
120, then 150, 180, and 220 depending on finish needs.
Wet or dry
Use dry sanding for furniture prep unless a specific coating step requires wet sanding.
Success check
Furniture surfaces and details feel uniformly smooth before coating.

What to do

  1. Inspect furniture for deep defects and raised edges.
  2. Use 120 grit for initial leveling.
  3. Progress to 150 and 180 grit across all surfaces.
  4. Use 220 grit for final prep before finish.
  5. Hand-sand details gently to match flat-area scratch pattern.
  6. Remove dust before applying stain or paint.
Avoid: Do not oversand edges, skip grit steps, or leave dust in joints and corners.
