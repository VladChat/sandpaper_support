Sanding leaves low spots

Some areas become lower or look dished after sanding.

Solution

Problem
Some areas become lower or look dished after sanding.
Likely cause
Pressure was focused in one area or a too-coarse grit removed material unevenly.
Recommended grit
Use the least aggressive grit that levels the defect, then refine.
Wet or dry
Dry or wet depending on surface and stage.
Success check
The surface looks and feels even before the next step.

Steps

  1. Clean the surface and the sheet.
  2. Use clean water and light pressure.
  3. Refine with the recommended grit sequence.
  4. Wipe and inspect before moving to the next step.
Avoid: Do not let dirty slurry or trapped grit stay under the sheet.
