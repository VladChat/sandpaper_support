Problem

What grit for sanding a wooden knife handle?

A wooden knife handle can feel rough, scratched, or unfinished after shaping.

wood dry sanding scratches coarse grit
Answer

For a wooden knife handle, shape with 120 or 180, then finish through 220, 320, and 400.

Why it happens
Handle wood needs shaping grits first, then finer grits for comfort before oil or finish.
Recommended grit
Use 120 or 180 for shaping, then 220, 320, and 400 for hand feel.
Wet or dry
Dry.
Success check
The handle feels smooth and comfortable with no sharp scratch lines.

What to do

  1. Shape rough areas with 120 or 180.
  2. Smooth with 220.
  3. Refine with 320 and 400.
  4. Remove dust before oil or finish.
Avoid: Do not leave coarse scratches where the hand grips the handle.
