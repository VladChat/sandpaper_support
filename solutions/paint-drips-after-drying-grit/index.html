Problem

What grit removes dried paint drips?

Dried paint drips leave raised bumps or ridges in the finish.

paint wet sanding dry sanding between coats
Answer

For dried paint drips, level with 320 or 400, then refine with 600 before recoating.

Why it happens
A drip must be leveled before the surface can be refined for repainting or polishing.
Recommended grit
Use 320 or 400 to level small dried drips, then 600 for finer prep.
Wet or dry
Dry for leveling; wet at 600 if the finish and stage allow it.
Success check
The drip is level and the repaired area blends into the surrounding finish.

What to do

  1. Let the paint dry or cure enough to sand cleanly.
  2. Level the drip with 320 or 400.
  3. Blend the surrounding area.
  4. Refine with 600 before recoating if needed.
Avoid: Do not sand soft paint drips that still smear.
